# Energy Management (Schwartz/Loehr)

## Overview

Performance depends not on time available but on energy available. Four dimensions of energy—physical (body), emotional (heart), mental (mind), and spiritual (purpose)—are independent resources that can be depleted and regenerated. A person can have 40 hours per week but perform poorly because one or more energy dimensions is bankrupt. Practitioners use this lens when productivity advice (better scheduling, more discipline, longer hours) fails and the real problem is that the person is running on fumes across one or more domains.

## Analytical Procedure

### Phase 1 — Energy Audit

1. **Identify the performance gap.** Describe the desired state (what should be happening) and the actual state (what is happening). Quantify if possible: "Should complete 3 features per sprint; currently completing 1.5."

2. **Rule out time as the primary constraint.** Ask: "If the person had 10 additional hours per week, would performance improve?" If no, time is not the bottleneck. Proceed to energy audit.

3. **For each of the four energy dimensions, inventory current depletion and regeneration practices.**

   **Physical Energy** (body)
   - Sleep: hours per night, consistency, quality (restless vs. deep)?
   - Exercise: frequency, intensity, type (aerobic vs. strength vs. flexibility)?
   - Nutrition: meal regularity, skipped meals, caffeine/alcohol use, hydration?
   - Recovery rituals: breaks during work, naps, rest days?

   **Emotional Energy** (heart)
   - Relationships: time spent with people who energize vs. drain?
   - Activities that trigger joy/engagement: how much time invested?
   - Stress triggers: unresolved conflicts, unsupportive environment, low autonomy?
   - Emotional expression: suppression patterns, outlets, processing habits?

   **Mental Energy** (mind)
   - Focus capacity: ability to concentrate on one task for 90+ minutes without distraction?
   - Cognitive load: number of simultaneous priorities, context-switching frequency?
   - Learning: time spent on growth, deliberate practice, or only reactive problem-solving?
   - Mental recovery: breaks from screens, media consumption, meditation, reading?

   **Spiritual Energy** (purpose)
   - Connection to mission: clarity on why the work matters?
   - Alignment: does daily work reflect stated values?
   - Contribution: sense of impact on something larger than self?
   - Meaning: absence of existential drift ("am I just going through motions")?

4. **Rate each dimension on a 0–10 scale.** 0 = completely depleted, 10 = thriving. Be honest.

5. **Identify which dimensions are below 6.** These are your energy leaks.

### Phase 2 — Cause Mapping

6. **For each depleted dimension, trace the immediate cause.** Do not attribute to character ("I'm lazy") or external circumstance alone ("My job is demanding"). Look for a mismatch between energy expenditure and regeneration.

   Example:
   - **Observation:** Physical energy = 4 (exhausted, poor sleep, no exercise).
   - **Expenditure:** 50-hour work weeks, frequent travel, irregular meals.
   - **Regeneration:** No structured sleep routine, skipped gym due to schedule pressure, coffee as meal replacement.
   - **Cause:** Regeneration practices are ad-hoc and fragile; disrupted by work demands. Sleep is not protected.

7. **Cross-check for cascading depletion.** Low physical energy often undermines emotional regulation. Low emotional energy often makes the work feel meaningless, draining spiritual energy. Identify which domino fell first.

### Phase 3 — Regeneration Design

8. **For each depleted dimension, design one regeneration ritual.** A ritual is a non-negotiable, repeating practice. It must be specific (day, time, duration) and small enough to start immediately.

   Examples:
   - **Physical:** 7am–7:30am, 3x/week: running (non-negotiable, blocked on calendar).
   - **Emotional:** Friday 6pm, 60 min: dinner with friend or family (protected).
   - **Mental:** 9am–9:50am before meetings start: uninterrupted focus work, no Slack (batched communication later).
   - **Spiritual:** Sunday evening 30 min: journaling on alignment between work and values.

9. **Test one ritual for 2–4 weeks.** Do not redesign your entire life. Pick the dimension with the most leverage (often physical, as it underpins the others) and establish the ritual before adding others.

10. **Measure energy change.** Re-rate the dimension after 2–4 weeks. If it improved ≥2 points, the ritual is working. If flat or declined, the ritual either doesn't match your regeneration need or is being sabotaged by schedule conflicts.

## Evaluation Criteria

| Check | Pass |
|---|---|
| All four energy dimensions were assessed with specific behaviors, not generic impressions | Y/N |
| At least two dimensions rated below 6 (otherwise no problem to solve) | Y/N |
| Energy depletion was distinguished from time scarcity | Y/N |
| For each depleted dimension, both expenditure and regeneration were inventoried | Y/N |
| At least one regeneration ritual is specific (day, time, duration, non-negotiable) | Y/N |
| Cascading effects across dimensions were examined (not just silos) | Y/N |

## Red Flags

- All dimensions rate 7+. Either the person is genuinely thriving (rare) or the inventory was too surface-level (common). Dig deeper into actual behaviors, not self-perception.
- A person claims "I don't have time for regeneration" without any ritual being attempted. This is not a time problem; it's a priority and belief problem. Rituals are small; what's really being said is "regeneration is not worth defending."
- The energy audit identifies a problem (e.g., "emotional energy is depleted because I'm in a toxic team") but the proposed ritual is a solo practice (e.g., "meditate alone"). Meditation might help resilience, but it does not regenerate emotional energy if the source of depletion remains active.
- Physical energy is rated low, but no sleep audit was done. Sleep is the load-bearing regeneration practice for all four dimensions. Without it, everything else is fragile.
- A ritual is designed but no measurement plan exists. "I'll know if I feel better" is not a signal. Define how the dimension will be re-rated in 2–4 weeks.

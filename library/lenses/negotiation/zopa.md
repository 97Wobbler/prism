# Zone of Possible Agreement

## Overview
ZOPA is the overlap between what each party is willing to accept. If Party A's minimum acceptable terms lie below Party B's maximum acceptable offer, a ZOPA exists — a deal is theoretically possible. If there is no overlap, the parties are negotiating across a gap that money or trade-offs alone cannot bridge. Practitioners map ZOPA to distinguish genuine impasse (no overlap exists) from negotiation theater (both sides are actually inside the zone but claiming otherwise). The discipline is precision about reservation prices and settlement ranges, not optimism about willingness to move.

## Analytical Procedure

### Phase 1 — Define the Dimension

1. **Identify the negotiable dimension.** Is this negotiation about price, timeline, scope, equity split, contract term, or something else? Write it plainly. If multiple dimensions exist (e.g., price AND start date), run this procedure separately for each dimension.

2. **Establish the metric.** What units measure this dimension? (e.g., dollars per unit, months, percentage equity, deliverables per quarter). Do not mix units.

### Phase 2 — Establish Reservation Prices (Your Side)

3. **Define your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement).** If you walk away from this deal, what happens? Quantify it on the dimension from Step 1. This is your reservation price — the boundary you will not cross without explicit escalation.

4. **Define your target.** What would you ideally like to achieve on this dimension? Write it.

5. **Define your walk-away price.** Below or above this price (depending on whether you are buying or selling), you refuse to negotiate further. This is your reservation price. Write it plainly.

### Phase 3 — Estimate Counterparty's Reservation Prices (Their Side)

6. **Gather signals about their BATNA.** What do you know or can you infer about what they will do if they walk away?
   - Have they stated it explicitly?
   - What alternatives are visible to them in the market?
   - What sunk costs or time pressure do they face?
   - What do past deals they've made tell you about their floor?

7. **Estimate their reservation price (lower bound of their acceptable range).** Write a range if certainty is low. This is the worst deal they will accept.

8. **Estimate their target (ideal outcome).** Infer from their opening bid, public statements, or market conditions.

9. **Estimate their walk-away price (upper bound of their range).** What is the best-case deal they would actually take?

### Phase 4 — Map the Zone

10. **Draw a one-dimensional number line.** Mark:
    - Your walk-away price (your floor)
    - Your target
    - Your reservation price
    - Their estimated walk-away price (their floor)
    - Their estimated target
    - Their estimated reservation price

11. **Identify the overlap.** The ZOPA is the region where your acceptable range intersects their acceptable range. If no overlap exists, there is no ZOPA — the deal is mathematically impossible without one party surrendering past its reservation price.

### Phase 5 — Test the Map

12. **Listen for signals that contradict your estimate.** In conversation, does the counterparty's stated floor move? Does new information emerge about their BATNA?

13. **Update your estimate of their zone.** Revise the boundaries if evidence warrants.

14. **Test the zone's size.** If the ZOPA is very small (e.g., a 1% price range), the deal is fragile — minor information or emotional shifts can collapse it. If it is large, there is room to trade across dimensions or to settle comfortably inside the zone.

## Evaluation Criteria

| Check | Pass |
|---|---|
| Negotiable dimension is stated as a single, measurable axis | Y/N |
| Your reservation price is specified (not a range, a single boundary) | Y/N |
| Your BATNA is quantified on the same dimension | Y/N |
| Counterparty's reservation price is estimated with stated reasoning | Y/N |
| A ZOPA is either identified or explicitly declared absent | Y/N |
| The size and robustness of the ZOPA is noted | Y/N |

## Red Flags

- Reservation price is stated as a range ("somewhere between X and Y"). A range is your negotiable zone; a reservation price is a boundary. Confusing them obscures your actual walk-away point.
- The "ZOPA" is asserted to exist without showing the number line. If you cannot draw it, it does not exist clearly enough to rely on.
- Counterparty's reservation price was guessed without evidence gathering. Estimates matter, but admit when they are thin.
- The ZOPA overlaps but is absurdly small (less than 1% of either party's starting position). This often signals a misdiagnosis — either the reservation prices are wrong or the negotiable dimension was defined too narrowly.
- Both parties claim their opening bid is already at their reservation price. One is lying, or both are negotiating the wrong dimension.
- The ZOPA was mapped once and never revisited as new information arrived. BATNAs change, market conditions shift, and reservation prices should be updated.

## Output Format

```
## ZOPA Analysis

**Negotiable Dimension:**
<stated as a single measurable axis>

### Your Side (Known)
- BATNA: <quantified outcome if deal fails>
- Target: <ideal outcome on this dimension>
- Reservation Price (Walk-away): <absolute boundary>

### Counterparty's Side (Estimated)
- Estimated BATNA: <inferred outcome if deal fails>
- Estimated Target: <inferred ideal outcome>
- Estimated Reservation Price (their floor): <inferred boundary>
- Reasoning for estimate: <what signals informed this>

### Number Line (Visual Map)
```
[their floor] ———— [target/zone] ———— [their ceiling]
                    [your floor] ———— [target/zone] ———— [your ceiling]
                                    ZOPA: [overlap region]
```

### ZOPA Status
- **Exists:** Yes / No
- **Size:** <quantified span, or "none">
- **Robustness:** <small/moderate/large — likelihood of collapse with new info>

### Updates & Signals
<Any revisions to the estimate based on conversation or new information>

### Confidence
<high/medium/low> — <justification for confidence in the reservation price estimates>
```
